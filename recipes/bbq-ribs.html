Smoked Baby Back BBQ Ribs

The Best Smoked BBQ Ribs You've Ever Tasted

Here is the real smoked ribs recipe, good enough to bring home a trophy in a cook-off.

They come off the grill tender yet pleasingly chewy, with just the right hit of smoky goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 slab ribs
  • 4 tablespoons of Meathead's Memphis Dust
  • 1 teaspoon of Kosher Salt
  • 6 tablespoons of Sweet Baby Ray's barbecue sauce

Steps

  1. Prep. Rinse the ribs in cool water to remove any bone bits from the butchering.
  2. Trim the excess fat from both sides of the rack of ribs.
  3. Next it is time to add the salt. You can simply eyeball it by sprinkling on the same amount of salt you would sprinkle on the ribs if they were served to you unsalted. If you can, give the salt 1 to 2 hours to be absorbed. The process of salting in advance is called dry brining.
  4. Applying the rubBefore applying the rub, just coat the meat with a thin layer of water.
  5. Fire up.Pre-heat your barbecue smoker. Bring the temperature to about 225Â°F and try to keep it there throughout the cook.
  6. Cook.Put the slabs in the cooker in indirect heat, meaty side up, close the lid, go read a book.
  7. For cooking time, allow 5 to 7 hours for St. Louis Cut (SLC) Ribs or Spare Ribs, and 3 to 5 hours for Baby Back Ribs.
